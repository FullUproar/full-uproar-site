Frequently Asked Questions

We believe in transparency. Most of these are real questions. Some of them we wish weren't.

Why was my patio extension denied?
Because it didn't meet community standards. If you need specifics, the Board reviewed your submission and determined that it was, and I quote the official minutes, "not in keeping with the aesthetic vision for Shady Pines." The President's patio extension was approved through a separate expedited process available to Board members. This is standard practice.
Can I see the minutes from the last Board meeting?
Minutes from all Board meetings are available upon request. Requests are processed by the Community Correspondence Review Sub-Committee, which meets twice yearly. Current processing time is 11 months. We appreciate your patience and remind you that patience is itself a community standard.
What happened to the Holiday Block Party fund?
The fund was reallocated to a critical landscaping initiative that benefits the entire community. The vendor was selected through a thorough process. The fact that the vendor shares a last name and home address with a Board member is coincidental and not relevant to this discussion.
Why can't I use the pool on weekends in July?
The Board has reserved weekend hours for strategic governance retreats, which are essential for maintaining community excellence. The agenda for these retreats is confidential. The inflatable flamingo visible from the parking lot is a Board-approved wellness tool.
How do I run for Board President?
Elections are held annually at a time and location to be determined by the current Board President. Candidates must submit a 15-page platform proposal, pass a community standards compliance audit (administered by the Board President), and collect signatures from 75% of residents. The current Board President ran unopposed. Twice. This is democracy working as intended.
Is the chili cook-off judging process fair?
Absolutely. The judging criteria ‚Äî taste, presentation, and "overall spirit" ‚Äî are evaluated by a panel of qualified experts. The panel currently consists of one expert. All scoring is final. The trophy is engraved in advance to save time, not because the outcome is predetermined. Next question.
What is impeachcolleen.com?
An unauthorized website created by disgruntled residents who have been fined a combined 47 times for legitimate violations. The Board's legal team is reviewing all options. We encourage residents not to visit the site at impeachcolleen.com, sign its so-called "petition," or share it on social media. Doing so may be considered "conduct detrimental to community harmony" under ¬ß18.
